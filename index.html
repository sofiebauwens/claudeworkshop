EBITDA Margin Collapse (2017–2024)
🏭 Client & Problem
  • European specialty & commodity chemicals producer, HQ Cologne, Germany
  • EBITDA margin collapsed from 24% → 7% between 2017 and 2024
  • EBIT margin fell from 19% → 1% — near breakeven
  • Driven by: energy cost spike (Russia/Ukraine), low utilisation (79% → 64% EU), compressed margins
  • EU carbon pricing now covers 28% of global GHG (rising to 44% by 2040)
❓ Three Strategic Questions
  • Q1 — What elements should AkChem consider when moving to sustainable chemicals?
  • Q2 — Which sustainable pathway should AkChem prioritise?
  • Q3 — Should AkChem close any fossil-based sites, and does switching make financial sense? (this dashboard)
⚖️ Regulatory Tailwinds
  • EU PPWR mandates 30–65% recycled content in packaging by 2030/2040
  • 66% of plastic-use companies have committed to ambitious GHG targets
  • Carbon pricing rising steeply — reaching ~44% of global GHG by 2040
  • Structural energy cost increase post-2022 raises fossil feedstock costs permanently
📐 DCF Model Assumptions
  • Baseline revenue = avg(2023, 2024, 2025) per site
  • Forecast horizon: 2026–2050 (25 years)
  • Fossil: +1%/yr revenue growth, no upfront investment
  • Sustainable: EUR 250M investment in 2026, +4%/yr growth from 2027
  • CapEx = 4.5% of revenue each year (both scenarios)
  • Depreciation ≈ ΔNWC → neither included in FCF
